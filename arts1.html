José T. Joya

Hills of Nikko

The painting is an allegory to human imperfections. The earth colors symbolize the weaknesses and limitations of humanity while blue represent life and white background represents the snow that starts to envelop the mound.

Fernando Amorsolo

Planting Rice

The painting captures the traditional way of planting rice in the Philippines, with men and women bent over in the fields, sowing the seeds. The lush greenery and the hardworking farmers in the painting symbolize the agricultural roots and the importance of rice cultivation in Filipino culture.

The Parisian Life

Botong Francisco

First Mass at Limasawa

Carlos Francisco's The First Mass at Limasawa is one of his finest artworks. This piece was commissioned by the national government to commemorate the 400 years of Philippine Christianization which was held in Cebu in 1965. It was also believed to be finished between 1950's.
