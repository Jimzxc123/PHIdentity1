Artist: Jose Joya (1931-1995) was a Filipino painter and pioneer of abstract expressionism. His work was known for its bold colors and loose, chaotic brushstrokes.

Significance: Joya donated Hills of Nikko to the National Museum of the Philippines in 1964, the same year it was completed. It was part of the Philippines' first entry to the Venice Biennale that year. The painting is now part of the National Fine Arts Collection and is exhibited at the Pillars of Philippine Modernism Gallery.
