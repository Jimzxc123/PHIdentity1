Spoliarium is a painting by Juan Luna that is considered a national symbol of the Philippines and a significant work of Philippine art from the 19th century:

What it depicts - The painting depicts the aftermath of a Roman gladiator match, with two fallen gladiators being dragged into the Spoliarium, the basement of the Roman Colosseum where the gladiators were stripped of their armor and weapons.

Why it's important - Luna created the painting to criticize the Spanish colonial government and the exploitation of Filipinos. It reflects the cultural and political context of the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period, and is considered a commentary on how the Spanish colonizers treated Filipinos.

Luna spent eight months painting Spoliarium on canvas. He submitted it to the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes in Madrid in 1884, where it won the first gold medal. In 1886, it was sold to the provincial government of Barcelona.

Spoliarium is one of the most famous and important paintings in Philippine art history, created by the renowned Filipino artist Juan Luna in 1884. This large-scale artwork won the gold medal at the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes (National Exhibition of Fine Arts) in Madrid, Spain, and it remains an iconic representation of both Filipino artistry and the country's struggle for freedom during the Spanish colonial era.

Today, Spoliarium is housed in the National Museum of Fine Arts in Manila, Philippines. It continues to be a source of pride for the Filipino people and a reminder of the country’s artistic heritage and its historical struggle for independence. The painting has become an iconic part of Filipino identity, representing both the nation's painful history and its eventual triumph over colonial oppression. In addition to its historical and cultural significance, Spoliarium has inspired countless artists, writers, and thinkers in the Philippines. It remains an enduring symbol of the power of art to provoke thought, inspire action, and reflect the collective consciousness of a nation.
